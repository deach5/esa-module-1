Informal risk assessment

  • Appropriate for low level risks or known risks with established and accepted control measures.
  • Can be done in your head or as part of a conversation.
  • May not always be documented.
  • Address the hazard or risk by immediately taking action.
  • Encourages us to Pause, Breathe and RESET and take time to stop, think, and consider the situation before acting.
  • Go-See walks.

Formal risk assessment

  • Required when a hazard or risk is more complex or does not have established and accepted control measures that can eliminate the hazard.
  • Risk assessment tools and templates are available to help site managers assess specific risks such as manual tasks and Floor Traffic Management Plans.
  • Complex risk assessments must be led by a suitably capable AP employee or delegate, such as a safety specialist.
  • Must be documented.
