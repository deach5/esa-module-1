Glossary

Term

Definition

Inclusive practices

A practice where all students of all capabilities have the opportunity to grow and learn.

Explicit
teaching

A way to teach in a direct, structured way.

High Impact Teaching Strategies

A bank of 10 instructional practices that are internationally recognised as some of the most reliable teaching strategies for delivering learning outcomes.

Discourse community

A group of people involved in and communicating about a particular topic, issue, or in a particular field.

Disposition

The tendency to respond to a situation. For example, having a positive attitude or perseverance.

Passive
learning

A method of learning where students receive information from the teacher and internalise it. The learner receives no feedback from the teacher.

Systematic instruction

Teaching that has a carefully planned sequence, including building from easier to more difficult tasks and breaking down harder skills into smaller parts.

Rote learning practices

A memorisation technique based on repetition.

Higher-order thinking

Thinking on a level that is higher than memorising facts or repeating something exactly the way it was told to you.

Productive struggle

The process of effortful learning that develops grit and creative problem solving.

Learning intentions

A statement, created by the teacher, that describes clearly what the teacher wants the students to know, understand, and be able to do as a result of learning and teaching activities.

Success
criteria

A set of features which a teacher wants to see in a student's work throughout a lesson or term.

Intuitive approaches

Intuitive approaches are typically fast and effective approaches used by students to solve problems. These approaches come from the students rather than from the teacher.

A studentâ€™s capacity to set targets, reflect on these and take ownership to realise the targets.

Cognitive activation

Teaching students skills and strategies that assist them to think deeply to find solutions and focus on methodology rather than just the answer.

Zone of proximal development

Defined to be the space between what a learner can do without assistance and what a learner can do with teacher or peer support.
